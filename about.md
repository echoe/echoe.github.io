Who am I? Well, funnily enough, I'm a person! In fact I am actually a /human being/ who /attempts to look like a person/ and /frequently fails/. Frequently I do a lot of things, and then none of them do anything specific. It all depends.

### My skills
I write, and I make music, and I code. Not necessarily in that order! BUT I WANT TO DO ALL OF THEM??? WHY IS LIFE FORSAKING ME????
One tries.

### What are your musical inspirations?
Hayley Kiyoko and Flying Lotus. Just imagine those two people crossed. That's who I want to be.
I mean, there are a ton of others though. Toro Y Moi, Tokimonsta, Syd tha Kid, Pentatonix ... etc. I ... I listen to a lot of music. Music is great. Kanye also, though ... sigh.

### What are your gaming inspirations?
If I could just replay Butterfly Soup every day for the rest of my life, I'd probably be happy. But eventually I'd make an extension where I could play as the goose from Untitled Goose Game, and every scene was just me honking in vain at the other protagonists.
I also play League of Legends. Way too much of it. It's bad.

### Who are your writing inspirations?

I'm going to be honest and say that David Foster Wallace inspired me a /lot/ when I was younger, and he still does today, despite the problems he's had. Ursula K. LeGuin is fantastic, I also read a lot of Kurt Vonnegut ... the entire Animorphs series fascinated me as a child, so K.A. Applegate. William Gibson is fantastic and Pattern Recognition is one of my favorite books for sure. Franz Kafka and Vladomir Nabokov. Cory Doctorow. Emily Dickinson, whose name I can never spell right.
... There are so many writers. It's hard to name them all; I think these are the biggest, but I'm not really sure. I've, ah, read a lot.

### Who are your coding inspirations?

I'll be honest, I don't think I have specific inspirations in regards to coding or designing systems! Maybe I should? It's hard to find these things out.

### Oh, okay. Well, what do you enjoy coding?
I make scripts that make things I'm doing better, basically. I would say I do less coding right now, and more ... designing systems to make things easier, whether that's coding or something else. Writing a lot of puppet code and terraform code, working within AWS. Adding build plans in Bamboo to automatically be able to update a consul installation I wrote, that works through Puppet to install servers and/or master installations. In general, just ... trying to use the right tool for the right job. And trying to convince others to do the same.

It's pretty tiring, though! I wish I could write more, but after coding/being the DevOps Holder of Many Hats, my brain tends to, ah, go 'splat'. Which isn't very conducive to writing.
